The ones I see for now are:

The fracture zone is creating K cuts: 
  1. The first cut is perpendicular to the distance between point of impact and centroid of asteroid; this creates a clean cut between the far side and a near side. Each of these cuts produces a far side; a convex polygon to be attached (in the next implementation step) to the surviving asteroid in a compound shape. Thus the surviving asteroid will most probably always be a concave compound shape.
  2. What remains of the near side after the K-1 cuts is then fractured with some other cuts, maybe all passing through the impact point, or maybe we'll consider other strategies for this part.
